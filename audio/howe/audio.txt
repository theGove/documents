When General Howe and the British Army captured Philadelphia, he set out to recruit those loyal to the crown to form a battalion of loyalists.  This handbill, used in recruiting efforts, laid out the benefits.  It promises that everyone who enlists and continues for the war's duration, will be awarded fifty acres of land in any county they choose, where, quote, Every gallant hero may retire, and enjoy his bottle and lass.



The Latin inscription at the top reads: with your great leader, there is nothing to fear.
