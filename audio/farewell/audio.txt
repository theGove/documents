As the first term of his presidency drew to a close, George Washington was ready to retire from public service. He began to draft a farewell address to be published in newspapers throughout the country, letting people know that he would not run for another term. 

However, two of his closest advisors, Alexander Hamilton and James Madison, convinced him to serve another term. Partway through his second term, he was determined to end after two terms, and with the help of Hamilton and Madison, he drafted his farewell address. 

It is widely recognized, to this day, as being among the best advice given to people for the purpose of self-government. 

The text was delivered to printer Septimus Claypool on Constitution Day, 1796. 

This document is the full newspaper printed from September 19, 1796. It is the first printing of George Washington's farewell address.
